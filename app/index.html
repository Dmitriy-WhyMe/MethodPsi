The earliest advocates of the method include the inventor of electroencephalography (EEG) which has today become neuroscience, Professor W. Grey Walter of the Burden Neurological Insitute at Bristol.

Other proponents for this have included two Nobel Prize Winners in the fields of biology and chemistry, Dr. Konrad Lorenz and Dr. Niko Tinbergen.
